#Prompt tips that help agents to generate better responses
## General tips:
    1. Be specific: Provide clear and detailed instructions to guide the agent's response. Avoid vague or ambiguous language that may lead to confusion.
    2. Use examples: Include examples in your prompt to illustrate the desired format or style of the response. This can help the agent understand your expectations better.
    3. Set constraints: If there are any limitations or requirements for the response, make sure to specify them in the prompt. This can help the agent generate a more relevant and accurate response.
    4. Ask open-ended questions: Encourage the agent to provide more comprehensive and thoughtful responses by asking open-ended questions. This can lead to more creative and insightful answers.
    5. Provide context: If the response requires background information or context, make sure to include it in the prompt. This can help the agent understand the situation better and generate a more informed response.
    6. Be concise: While it's important to provide enough information, try to keep the prompt concise and to the point. Avoid unnecessary details that may distract the agent from the main focus of the response.
   
## Effective Prompts
After any documentation creation, ask for a clean-up step.  
    Review the document. Remove redundancies, ambiguities, and fabrications. Make sure the statements are clear and crisp without removing intent.

Tell the agent to make a plan and keep track of it's work.
    Complete the requested task. Start by creating a short task list with concrete steps. Keep exactly one step in-progress at a time and update the list whenever a step is completed, added, removed, or blocked. If new information changes the scope, revise the plan before continuing. Keep progress updates brief and milestone-based. Finish by validating the work and reporting the completed steps, checks performed, and any remaining risks or blockers.

    Prefer the smallest correct change that satisfies the request.
    
    Do not make unrelated edits, refactors, or dependency changes unless required.
